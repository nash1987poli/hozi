## Part 2 — Live walkthrough ("if they ask you to show it")

Speak these lines, click in this order. ~90 seconds. This is your safety net.

1. **Open (default view).** "This is Hozi. It shows where every district stands today — June — with a ranked watch-list on the right."
2. **Point to the hero line.** "The headline: without early action, 19 of our 20 districts are projected into high food-security risk by September."
3. **Press Play (or drag the timeline).** "Watch the season turn — risk spreads up from the dry south. July to September are forecasts."
4. **Click the top district.** "Tap any district and it shows why — rainfall, drying crops, the trajectory. No black box."
5. **Response Planner — drag support to ~7.** "Now the important part. Say we can support a handful of districts this season. Hozi shows where that effort saves the most — driest, least-served first. It never sets the budget; it stretches the one you have."
6. **Point at the stats.** "Two districts kept out of high risk, 80-odd risk points removed — instantly."
7. **Scroll to the data pipeline.** "Here's how it's fed — rainfall, satellite, Agritex, markets, district reports. Data stays with its owners; Hozi just reads the signals."
8. **Toggle chiShona, then the honesty panel.** "It speaks our languages — and it's honest about what it can't know. Decision-support, not a crystal ball."

**15-second version:** default → Play (red spread) → Response Planner drag → "see the crisis coming, and know where to act."

## Part 3 — Judge Q&A cheat-sheet

- **"How accurate is it?"** — "It validates at r=0.81 against an independent risk index, so the model reflects real conditions. The forecast projects a known seasonal trend with an honest confidence band — decision-support, not certainty."
- **"Isn't this just WFP HungerMap?"** — "HungerMap monitors, country-level, globally. Hozi is district-level, Zimbabwean-owned, and it *plans* — it tells you where to act, which HungerMap doesn't."
- **"Where does the data come from?"** — (point to the pipeline) "Met Services, satellites, Agritex, markets, district officers — and Project Pangolin long-term. Owners keep control."
- **"Are you telling government how to spend?"** — "No. Hozi never sets budgets. It helps officials stretch resources they've already been given, and defend their own decisions."
- **"What does it cost to run?"** — "Tiny — runs in a normal browser on open data. Going live is connecting feeds, not rebuilding."
- **"Why back it?"** — "It's the first sovereign, open, Zimbabwean-owned food-security foresight tool — and it's already working."
