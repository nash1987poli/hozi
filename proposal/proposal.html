The problem

We see the hunger coming. We just see it too late to act in time.

Almost every year, parts of Zimbabwe slide into food shortage when the rains fail and harvests fall short. The tragedy is rarely that no one saw it coming. It is that the warning arrives too late, buried in slow seasonal reports produced outside the country — after the season is already lost and the moment for early action has passed.

Planners are then left to stretch limited resources across many struggling districts with no quick, shared way to see where action would do the most good. The result is help that arrives late, and in the wrong order.

The solution

Hozi — from early warning to a ready plan.

Hozi does two things no seasonal report does. First, it forecasts where food risk is heading over the next one to three months, for every district. Then it helps responders plan: enter the resources already set aside, and Hozi shows where that effort protects the most people and hectares — so existing budgets go further.

Who it is for — and how it respects their authority

Hozi is a decision-support tool for the people who plan the response — the Ministry of Lands & Agriculture, food-security and civil-protection officers, ZimVAC, and relief NGOs. It supports existing decision-makers and processes; it informs, it does not decide or override. Hozi never sets budgets and never tells government how to spend. The planner is always in control.

Technical specification

A transparent engine — not a black box.

How it is built

  • Data layer: district climate, crop & market signals (rainfall, NDVI vegetation, pests, irrigation, input access, prices, yields).
  • Risk engine: a clear weighted model giving a 0–100 food-security risk per district.
  • Forecast: seasonal trend projection 1–3 months ahead, with a widening confidence band.
  • Response planner: for a given level of resources, ranks where action reduces the most risk.
  • Interface: a web map — time-slider, district drill-down, what-if planner — on a normal browser.

Proven against the data

0.81
Match between Hozi's risk score and an independent agricultural risk index

A strong match means the model reflects real conditions — it is not guessing. From there, Hozi projects the same well-understood seasonal trend forward and is honest about uncertainty.

The feature that sets Hozi apart

The Response Planner — make every committed dollar count

Enter the resources already allocated for the season and Hozi shows where that effort protects the most people per dollar, and in what order. Global tools forecast hunger; Hozi helps our own responders turn an existing budget into the most lives protected — without ever setting that budget.

Projected benefits

Every $1 of early action saves $7. Hozi helps find it in time.

The World Food Programme estimates anticipatory action returns at least seven-to-one. The hard part is acting early and in the right order. In a working test on national sample data, Hozi flagged a sharp seasonal rise in risk and showed where existing effort would pay off most.
