Solution Proposal · 02
The problem

We see our crises coming. We just see them too late to act.

Drought and hunger, floods, disease outbreaks — Zimbabwe's biggest shocks are rarely a surprise. The signs build for weeks. Yet the warning usually arrives too late, buried in slow reports produced outside the country, after the season is lost. And even when a district is flagged, planners must stretch limited resources with no quick, shared way to see where action does the most good.

The solution

Hozi — from early warning to a ready plan.

Hozi forecasts where risk is heading, district by district, then helps responders plan: enter the resources already set aside, and it shows where that effort protects the most people. Built first for food security.

One engine, many national risks

The pattern is always the same — signals → risk score → forecast → response plan. Build it once, apply it everywhere:

This is the Strategy's called-for "AI disaster-warning system", built as reusable national infrastructure — not a single-use app.

Who it is for — and how it respects their authority

Hozi is a decision-support tool for those who plan the response: the Ministry of Lands & Agriculture, food-security and civil-protection officers, ZimVAC, health authorities and relief NGOs. It supports existing decision-makers; it informs, it does not decide or override. Hozi never sets budgets and never tells government how to spend. The planner is always in control.

In one line: the world's tools tell Zimbabwe what will happen. Hozi helps our own responders act on it in time, with the resources they already have — across every risk that matters, and it's ours.

Technical specification

A transparent engine — powered by AI where AI is strong.

How it is built

  • Data layer: district climate, crop & market signals — auto-fed from live feeds.
  • Risk engine: a clear, weighted, transparent model — a 0–100 risk per district.
  • Forecast: seasonal projection 1–3 months ahead with an honest confidence band.
  • Response Planner: ranks where a given level of support cuts the most risk.
  • Interface: a web map — time-slider, drill-down, planner — on any browser.

The AI language layer

We keep AI out of the math (so numbers stay auditable) and use it where it excels — language: it writes plain-language early-warning briefs in English, chiShona & isiNdebele, answers natural-language questions ("which districts need seed first?"), and turns messy field reports into clean signals.

Automated & lean (n8n)

The whole pipeline runs on n8n automation: it pulls new data on a schedule, refreshes the forecast, and pushes local-language alerts to district officers by WhatsApp — so a small team can run it nationally, and it updates itself.

The feature that sets Hozi apart

The Response Planner — make every committed dollar count

Enter the resources already allocated and Hozi shows where they protect the most people per dollar, driest and least-served first. Global tools forecast the crisis; Hozi helps our own responders turn an existing budget into the most lives protected — without ever setting that budget.

Projected benefits

Every $1 of early action saves $7. Hozi helps find it in time.

The World Food Programme estimates anticipatory action returns at least seven-to-one — the hard part is acting early and in the right order. In a working test on national sample data, Hozi flagged a sharp seasonal rise in risk and showed where existing effort would pay off most.
